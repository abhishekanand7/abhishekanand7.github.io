Abhishek Anand

I am a postdoctoral research scientist in Westervelt Aerosol Group in Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory at Columbia University. I am building machine/deep learning models for estimating air pollutant using large datasets from NASA's and European satellite fleet, validated with ground-level sensors in sub-Saharan Africa. I am further using the datasets to investigate health impacts to aid effective local policymaking.

I graduated with Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Carnegie Mellon University, where I worked with Prof. Albert Presto on developing low-cost techniques to measure atmospheric particulate matter and using air pollution data from low-cost monitors to identify emission sources. Additionally, I built a deep learning-based forecast model for fine particulate matter over Pittsburgh (USA) by using novel features from NASA's GEOS-CF and ground level measurements from a low-cost sensor network, spatially deployed over Pittsburgh in Pennsylvania.

I earned my M.Phil. and M.Sc. from Hong Kong University of Science and Technology and B.Tech. in Civil Engineering from Indian Institute of Technology Delhi in New Delhi, India.

Research Interests

I'm interested in application of image processing and machine learning on remote sensing datasets, atmospheric simulations, and investigating health impacts from air pollution.
